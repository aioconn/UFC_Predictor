Picking UFC Fight Winners Using Predictive Modeling

UFC Background Knowledge

Before we dive into this topic here is some quick background information just in case you do not know what UFC or what the rules are. UFC (Ultimate Fighting Championship) is a MMA (mixed martial arts) organization that showcases bouts (fights) between two fighters. The main rules are the fighters can both strike and wrestle, some strikes and wrestling positions are deemed to be illegal resulting in disqualifications, and the bouts last for 3 five-minute rounds or 5 five-minute rounds if it is a championship fight. Finally, the winner is determined by KO (knockout), submission, decision if they ,make it through all rounds, a draw, disqualification, or a doctor stoppage.

Introduction

One of my favorite parts of the sport is analyzing the fighter statistics and trying to predict who will likely win. If I like my analysis, I sometimes even bet on that fighter, however this always seems to be a hit or a miss. One problem with this is my own biases get in the way of my analysis, if I really like a certain fighter, I can easily overlook what their opponent brings to the table. Also, there is so much information that I can make a prediction over that I might not weigh the correct ones that will make a difference in the fight. So, is it possible to correctly predict the winner of a UFC fight every time? The answer is no, simply because upsets in sports happen and anything can happen in a fight. However, in this blog I am going to use predictive modeling techniques to help predict the winners of these matches. Even though it might be impossible to predict every fight correctly, if I could find a technique that results in predicting the outcome correctly 65%-70% of the time, then I think it will be a success.

The data I used for my predictive model is from here https://www.kaggle.com/rajeevw/ufcdata This dataset had all the information that I thought would be needed to make an accurate prediction for a fight, including individual fighter stats like height, reach, and age, but all fight statistics like their striking percentages and takedown defense percentages. Also it included more advanced fighter statistics such as how many loses or wins they have had in a row which I felt would be very important for decision making.

I first filtered out rows that had any cells empty, since adding any type of false data would most likely make the prediction less accurate. I will be using a random forest model for my predictive model and I will use the dataset as training data. I will then use my model on the test data that is from a UFC card on 5/1/2021. If the prediction has above a 65% prediction of winning, I will theoretically bet on that fighter, at the end of the card I will see how much I have won. I could also use fights from the past to see who will when and see how accurate the model is from that as well.
